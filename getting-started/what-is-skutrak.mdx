SkuTrak is a **demand intelligence platform** for FMCG companies. It collects data from your retailers, standardises it, and gives you dashboards and tools to understand what's happening with your products on shelf, in depot, and at the till.

## What it actually does

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Collects your retailer data" icon="plug">
    SkuTrak connects to retailer portals and pulls your data automatically. No more logging into five different systems every Monday morning.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Standardises everything" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Different retailers report differently. SkuTrak normalises the data so you can compare Tesco and Sainsbury's side by side without a spreadsheet.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Shows you what matters" icon="chart-line">
    Dashboards built for the questions you actually ask: are sales up or down, is availability healthy, where are the gaps in supply?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Helps you act on it" icon="bullseye">
    Alerts flag problems early. Distribution tools show where you're gaining or losing stores. Promotion reviews show what worked and what didn't.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Who uses it

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    You use SkuTrak to prepare for buyer meetings, track SKU performance, and build evidence for range reviews and distribution requests. The dashboards give you the numbers. The knowledge base teaches you how to read them.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Supply Chain Teams">
    You use SkuTrak to monitor depot stock, track inbound service levels, and catch availability problems before they hit the shelf. Daily data means you can act on Monday, not find out on Friday.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Commercial and Category Teams">
    You use SkuTrak to review promotions, model distribution changes, and understand what's driving demand across your category. The commercial dashboards connect sales outcomes to the levers you control.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Retailer coverage

SkuTrak connects to major UK and US retailers including Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda, Co-op, Waitrose, Morrisons, Ocado, Amazon, Home Depot, and others. Each retailer connection collects different metrics depending on what that retailer makes available to suppliers.

<Tip>
  Check the **Data Sources** section in your account to see which retailers you're connected to and whether your connections are healthy.
</Tip>
