The Top Line Report is your **weekly starting point**. It answers one question: *"Is my account growing or shrinking, and what's causing it?"*

It gives you a unified view of sales value, rate of sale, availability, and supply chain health — all compared against previous periods so you can spot changes before they become problems.

## Why it matters

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    This is your preparation for **buyer calls and range reviews**. Before any conversation, you can verify top SKU performance and identify red flags — so you walk in with evidence, not assumptions.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Supply Chain Analysts">
    This is your **early warning system**. Sales declines almost always start as supply issues. Gaps between DC and store inbound typically precede shelf shortages by **1–2 weeks** — giving you a window to act.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## When to use it

| Trigger | What you're checking |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Weekly check-in** (Mondays) | Overall account health — are we up or down? |
| **Before a buyer meeting** | SKU-level performance to support your talking points |
| **When an alert triggers** | Root cause — is the issue demand, supply, or distribution? |

## What to look for

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Sales declining, availability stable" icon="chart-line-down">
    This is a **demand problem**, not a supply problem. Check rate of sale and look for distribution losses. The product is on shelf — shoppers just aren't buying it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="DC inbound falling, store inbound stable" icon="warehouse">
    Retailers deplete depot stock first. This pattern creates a **1–2 week window** before shelf gaps appear. Act now — by the time store inbound drops, you're already losing sales.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Sales growth exceeding rate of sale growth" icon="chart-line-up">
    This signals **distribution gains** — your product is reaching more stores. This is strong evidence for range expansion discussions with buyers.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to read it

Follow this 60-second sequence each time you open the report:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Scan the metric table">
    Look at the colour coding — **green means growth**, **red means decline**. This gives you the headline in seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the trend line">
    Is the direction improving or worsening? Compare against the prior year line to separate seasonal patterns from real change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the product breakdown">
    The bar chart shows SKU-level drivers. Identify which products are pulling performance up or dragging it down.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cross-reference supply metrics">
    Compare DC inbound and store inbound against sales. If supply metrics are declining but sales haven't dropped yet, you have a narrow window to intervene.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Dashboard controls

The toolbar at the top of the report has four settings:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Product Grouping" icon="layer-group">
    Switch between Categories, Subcategories, Brands, or individual SKUs. Default: **SKU**.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Select Metric" icon="gauge">
    Choose which metric appears in the bar chart and time series. Default: **Sales Value**.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Time Period" icon="calendar">
    Set the timeframe for the bar chart and trend line. Default: **Year to Date**.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Comparison Period" icon="calendar-check">
    Set the baseline for comparison. Selecting "Previous" compares against the directly preceding period of the same length.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What's on screen

The report has three visual sections that work together:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Product Breakdown Bar Chart">
    The first bar displays the total for your primary metric in the chosen period. The second bar shows the **variance** between the selected and comparison periods — so you can see the gap at a glance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Metric Table">
    Shows **all metrics** across available time periods, with selected period totals and percentage variance from your comparison period. This is where you get the full picture.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Time Series">
    Charts your primary metric over the chosen period. The **blue line** is the current period; the **grey shaded area** is the comparison period. Crossovers and divergences are what matter here.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Quick reference

| Metric | Category | What it tells you |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Sales Value** | Commercial | Total revenue from your products at this retailer |
| **Rate of Sale** | Commercial | Units sold per store per week — rising means stronger demand |
| **DC Inbound** | Supply Chain | Percentage of ordered cases delivered to the retailer's distribution centres |
| **Store Inbound** | Supply Chain | Percentage of orders fulfilled from DC to store |
| **Availability** | Critical | Percentage of stores stocking your product |
| **Waste Value** | Supply Chain | Revenue lost to markdown or disposal |

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Start broad, then drill down. Use Product Grouping to look at category-level trends first, then switch to SKU level once you've identified which category needs attention.
</Tip>
