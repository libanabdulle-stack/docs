The Depot Stock report shows you **what's sitting in the retailer's distribution centres**. It tracks stock units, weeks of cover, and inbound trends so you can see whether supply is healthy or heading for a gap before it affects store availability.

## Why it matters

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Supply Chain Analysts">
    This is your **forward-looking indicator**. Store availability is a lagging metric, it tells you what already went wrong. Depot stock tells you what's **about to go wrong**. If stock is running low at DC level, you have a 1-2 week window before stores start going empty.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    You don't need to manage supply chain day-to-day, but you need to **know when there's a problem coming**. If depot stock is low on your top SKUs before a key trading period, that's a conversation to have with your supply chain team now, not after the lost sales show up.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## When to use it

| Trigger | What you're checking |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Weekly supply review** | Are stock levels healthy across your range? |
| **DC inbound dropped on Top Line Report** | How much stock is left in the depot? How many weeks of cover? |
| **Ahead of a promotional period** | Is there enough stock in the pipeline to support the uplift? |
| **After a supply disruption** | Have stock levels recovered, or are depots still depleted? |

## What to look for

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Weeks of cover dropping below 2" icon="clock">
    This is the danger zone. Most retailers need **2-3 weeks of depot cover** to maintain store availability. Below 2 weeks, stores will start running out. Below 1 week, gaps are almost guaranteed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Stock levels falling while sales are stable" icon="arrow-trend-down">
    If sales haven't changed but depot stock is declining, **inbound supply isn't keeping pace with demand**. Check whether deliveries have been missed, reduced, or delayed. This is an upstream problem.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Stock building up with no increase in sales" icon="boxes-stacked">
    Rising depot stock without rising sales suggests **over-supply or a demand slowdown**. Watch for waste risk, especially on short shelf-life products. This could also signal a future markdown.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to read it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check weeks of cover across your range">
    This is the most important number. Sort by weeks of cover ascending to find the SKUs closest to running out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compare against inbound trends">
    Is stock being replenished? If weeks of cover is low but inbound is increasing, the situation is recovering. If inbound is also declining, the problem is getting worse.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flag anything below 2 weeks cover">
    These products need immediate attention. Cross-reference with your delivery schedule to understand when the next inbound is expected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check high-value SKUs separately">
    Your top 10 SKUs by sales value deserve individual attention. A stock gap on a top seller costs disproportionately more than on a tail SKU.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Quick reference

| Metric | Category | What it tells you |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Depot Stock Units** | Supply Chain | Total units held at the retailer's distribution centres |
| **Weeks of Cover** | Critical | How many weeks current stock would last at the current rate of sale. Below 2 is a red flag |
| **DC Inbound %** | Supply Chain | Percentage of ordered cases actually delivered to the DC |
| **Store Outbound** | Supply Chain | Volume leaving the DC for stores. Rising outbound with flat inbound depletes stock |
| **Stock Build** | Supply Chain | Whether stock is accumulating or depleting over time |
| **Days Since Last Delivery** | Supply Chain | How recently the DC received your product. Useful for spotting delivery gaps |

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Set a personal threshold of 2.5 weeks of cover as your early warning line. By the time stock hits 2 weeks, you're already in reactive mode. At 2.5 weeks you still have time to chase a delivery or escalate with your supply team.
</Tip>
