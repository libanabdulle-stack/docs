<Tip>
Every retailer gives your products different codes. Tesco says `5012345678`, your system says `HUM-200G-CL`. The Product Catalogue links them so your dashboards work. If products aren't mapped, they're invisible — your sales look lower than they are, and your buyer meeting starts with wrong numbers.
</Tip>

## Who is this for?

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    Before your next buyer review, check your mapping coverage. Unmapped products mean understated sales — you're selling yourself short to your buyer. Fix it before the meeting, not during.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Supply Chain">
    Unmapped products don't appear in inbound or availability dashboards. If DC Inbound looks fine but stores are out of stock, check whether new SKUs have been mapped. A missing mapping is a blind spot.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Data Managers">
    The Product Catalogue replaces your spreadsheet. AI suggestions handle most new products — you review and confirm. Every change is tracked with full audit history.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## What goes wrong without clean mappings?

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="New product, not mapped" icon="eye-slash">
    It won't appear in any dashboard. Your buyer sees zero sales for a product that's actually selling. You lose credit for a successful launch.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Wrong mapping" icon="shuffle">
    Sales get attributed to the wrong SKU. Category mix and promo analysis become unreliable. You can't trust the numbers you're presenting.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mapping deleted" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    Historical data disappears from views. Year-on-year comparisons break silently. You'll spend hours figuring out why last year's numbers don't match.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How do I fix it?

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check your mapping coverage">
    Open the Product Catalogue and look at the coverage percentage per retailer. Anything below 95% needs attention. [View the Product Catalogue →](/using-skutrak/product-definitions)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review AI suggestions">
    SKUtrak suggests the most likely match for new products as they appear. Review the suggestions queue weekly — most are correct, you just need to confirm. [How to review suggestions →](/using-skutrak/map-retailer-codes)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map remaining products manually">
    For new launches or retailer exclusives the AI can't match, search by barcode or description and link them yourself. [How to map products →](/using-skutrak/map-retailer-codes)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add pricing data">
    List prices, customer discounts, and production costs sync with SKUtrak Promote. Do it once per product and every promotional ROI calculation is automatic. [Manage pricing →](/using-skutrak/pricing-and-terms)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**Weekly habit** — Check the Product Catalogue every Monday morning. It takes 2 minutes and catches unmapped products before they create dashboard gaps. Most teams assign one person to own this — it's a small job with outsized impact.
</Note>
