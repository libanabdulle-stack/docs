<Tip>
Retailers use their own product codes. Tesco might call your hummus `5012345678`, Sainsbury's calls it `SA-HUM-001`. The Product Catalogue's Retailer Mappings page links each retailer code to your internal definition so the data shows up correctly in dashboards. Three options: upload a CSV of known mappings, use Quick Match for AI-assisted bulk mapping, or map products one by one.
</Tip>

## Who is this for?

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Account Managers">
    You probably won't map products yourself, but you should know how to spot gaps. If a product is missing from your Top Line report, check the Product Catalogue's unmapped list first. If it's there, map it or flag your data manager.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Supply Chain">
    New retailer SKUs appear regularly — promotions, range extensions, pack changes. If they're not mapped, they don't appear in supply dashboards. Make mapping part of your new product launch process.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Data Managers">
    This is your most frequent Product Catalogue task. Aim to clear the unmapped queue weekly. Quick Match handles most of it in under 5 minutes.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Where do I manage mappings?

Go to **Product Catalogue > Retailer Mappings**. You'll see two tabs:

| Tab | What it shows |
|-----|--------------|
| **Unmapped Retailer Products** | Products the retailer has reported that don't yet have a link to your internal definitions |
| **Mappings** | All confirmed retailer-to-internal product links |

## How do I upload mappings via CSV?

Best for bulk onboarding or when your mappings already exist in a spreadsheet.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your CSV">
    You need two columns at minimum: the retailer SKU code and your internal unique code. Both must already exist in SKUtrak — the retailer SKU from the data feed, and the unique code from your product definitions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the file">
    Above the Unmapped Retailer Products table, click **Upload Matches**. Click **Upload File**, select your CSV, and click **Confirm**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map the columns">
    Match each column in your file to the correct field. Click **Upload**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the results">
    A report shows how many mappings loaded successfully. If any failed, review the error messages, correct the affected rows, and re-upload.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
If a retailer product already has a mapping, uploading a new one will overwrite it. Double-check your file if you're re-uploading to avoid accidentally breaking existing mappings.
</Warning>

## How do I use Quick Match?

Best for weekly maintenance when new retailer products appear. SKUtrak suggests the most likely match based on descriptions, barcodes, and past patterns.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Filter your list">
    Use the filters on the Unmapped Retailer Products page to narrow by retailer, category, or other criteria. This makes the queue more manageable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch Quick Match">
    Click **Quick Match** above the table. A pop-up shows the first unmapped retailer product with suggested matches from your product definitions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm or search">
    Select the correct match from the suggestions. If it's not listed, use the search bar. Still can't find it? Click **Create Product** to add a new definition on the spot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move through the queue">
    Click **Save and Next** to confirm and move on. Use **Skip** for any products you want to come back to later. When finished, click **Save and Close**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**Speed tip** — Filter by a single retailer before launching Quick Match. The suggestions are more accurate when the context is narrower, and you can clear an entire retailer's queue in one sitting.
</Note>

## How do I map a single product?

Best for one-off corrections or when you spot a specific unmapped product.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the product">
    In the Unmapped Retailer Products table, tick the checkbox next to the product you want to map.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the mapping pop-up">
    In the blue table header, click **Match**. Choose the correct product definition from the suggestions, or search for it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Save**. The product moves to the Mappings table.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What if a mapping is wrong?

If a retailer product was linked to the wrong internal definition, you have two options:

- **Re-upload via CSV** — uploading a new mapping for the same retailer SKU overwrites the old one
- **Delete and re-map** — remove the incorrect mapping from the Mappings table, then map the product again using Quick Match or the individual method

<Check>
You've mapped products correctly when: the Unmapped Retailer Products count drops, and the coverage percentage on the Product Catalogue page rises. Aim for 95%+ per retailer.
</Check>
